Circle Packing

Division >> Battalion >> Station Area

Encoding

The red color encodes the number of fire department call for service that went to a given station. The depth is encoded by the Viridis color scale, but has no useful data other than the differentiate them from each other and from the the leaf node color scale. The circle placement encode a parent to child relationship by representing a "belongs to" relationship. The size also encodes the number of fire department call for service that went to a given station.

Interactivity

This visualization support details on demand through tooltips. The tooltip shows the node that is being hovered over as well as the count and level (depth).

Conclusion

It is even easier to see here (versus the dendrogram) that Station Area 1 has the highest count. We can also see that Division 2 and 3 have about the comparable counts while the Station Areas that fall outside of that range are much smaller.
